Information

Video games can play an important role in the development of students as extra-curricular activities in school. Not only do they provide a fun and engaging form of entertainment, but they can also help to improve various skills such as problem-solving, critical thinking, and teamwork. Additionally, many modern video games are designed to challenge players and require a high level of strategic thinking, which can help to develop critical thinking skills that can be applied to other areas of life. Furthermore, playing video games in a school setting can promote socialization and teamwork among students, as they often require communication and collaboration to achieve goals. This not only helps to foster positive relationships between students, but also provides opportunities for students to develop important life skills that will be valuable to them in their future careers and personal lives.

Our Goal

Our goal is to promote the integration of esports into schools as a recognized and valuable extracurricular activity. We believe that esports provides students with unique opportunities for teamwork, strategic thinking, and problem solving, and should be treated with the same level of respect as traditional sports. By advocating for esports in schools, we hope to create a supportive environment that encourages students to pursue their interests, develop critical skills, and build meaningful relationships with their peers.
